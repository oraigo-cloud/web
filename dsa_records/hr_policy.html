Online Training Program — HR Policies

Organization: DataSphere Academy  •  Effective Date: Nov 01 2025

Document ID: HR_POLICY  •  Version: 1.0

Purpose & Scope

This document defines the HR policies for the organization's fully online training programs. It applies to all employees, contractors, and trainees participating in virtual training sessions, e-learning modules, or assessments delivered by the organization.

1. Eligibility

  • All employees and assigned trainees are required to participate in the online training modules relevant to their role or onboarding plan.
  • Eligibility is determined by role requirements, manager recommendation, or HR assignment.
  • Participants must have reliable internet access and a functional device capable of running the required training platform(s).

2. Attendance & Participation

  • Attendance at scheduled virtual sessions is mandatory unless prior approval is obtained from HR or the training coordinator.
  • Participants should join sessions on time, remain present for the duration, and actively engage with the trainer and peers.
  • Excessive tardiness or unexplained absences may result in removal from the program or withholding of certification.

3. Code of Conduct

  • Participants must maintain professional behavior during all online sessions and communications.
  • Cameras are expected to be on where practical; if a camera cannot be used, participants should inform the trainer in advance.
  • Use chat and collaboration tools respectfully and only for training-related discussion.
  • Use of offensive language, harassment, or disruptive behavior will result in disciplinary action.

4. Evaluation & Certification

  • Participants will be evaluated through online quizzes, assignments, or project submissions as defined per module.
  • A minimum passing score of 80% is required to qualify for certification.
  • Certificates will be issued only after full completion of required sessions and successful evaluation.
  • Participants who fail to achieve 80% may be offered one retake attempt, at HR's discretion. Additional remedial support may be provided.

5. Technical Requirements & Support

  • Participants are responsible for their own internet connectivity and device readiness.
  • The organization will provide access credentials and instructions for approved platforms (LMS, video conferencing tools, collaboration apps).
  • Technical support is available during working hours for access or platform-related issues; participants must report problems promptly.

6. Confidentiality & Intellectual Property

  • All training materials, recordings, slides, and resources are the property of the organization.
  • Participants may not record, copy, share, or redistribute training materials or session links without written authorization.
  • Work, projects, or solutions developed during training are considered company intellectual property unless otherwise stated.

7. Feedback & Continuous Improvement

  • Participants are required to complete feedback forms at the end of each module to help improve content and delivery.
  • HR will review feedback and implement improvements for future cohorts where applicable.

8. Equal Opportunity & Accessibility

  • The organization ensures equal access to training for all eligible participants regardless of background, gender, or location.
  • Reasonable digital accommodations (e.g., captions, accessible files) will be provided upon request.
