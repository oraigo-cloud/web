Live vs. Dev Site Consistency

This section outlines the QA approach to ensure the new WordPress development site is consistent with the live site in terms of navigation, content, links, functionality, design, and performance.

1. Checklist for Comparing Live vs. Dev Site

A. Navigation & Structure

  • All main and sub-navigation items match (labels, order, destination URLs).
  • Footer menus and links are identical.
  • Internal linking between pages is consistent.

B. Page Content

  • Page titles, headings (H1–H3), and body text match.
  • Images and videos are correct and load properly.
  • Alt text, captions, and formatting are consistent.
  • Blog posts, dates, and categories are correct.

C. Links

  • All internal/external links point to correct destinations.
  • No broken or redirected links.
  • Buttons lead to correct pages or forms.

D. Features & Functionality

  • Contact forms, search, sliders, and pop-ups work as expected.
  • Plugins (e.g., galleries, before/after sliders) behave the same.
  • Interactive elements (hover effects, modals, accordions) function consistently.

E. Design & Layout

  • Fonts, colors, spacing, and logo placement match.
  • Responsive behavior (mobile/tablet/desktop) mirrors the live site.
  • No alignment or overflow issues.

F. Performance

  • Page load speed is comparable or improved on the dev site.
  • No console or JavaScript errors.

2. Handling Discrepancies

  • Log each difference with screenshots and page URLs.
  • Categorize discrepancies:
    • Expected Change: Approved improvements or redesigns.
    • Unintended Difference: Must be fixed to match the live site.
  • Prioritize fixes by impact:
    • High: Broken functionality or navigation.
    • Medium: Content mismatch or design inconsistency.
    • Low: Minor visual difference or spacing.
  • Communicate with client and dev team — confirm which changes are intentional before fixing.
  • Re-test after fixes to ensure consistency is restored.
